## Growth Engine

### 1. New Customer Acquisition
- Set monthly lead generation targets by channel: direct outreach, referrals, partners, digital inbound.
- Track conversion through every stage: lead, qualified, proposal, negotiation, closed won.
- Maintain enough top-of-funnel activity to sustain at least `3x` weighted pipeline for the next quarter.
- Review lost deals every month and categorize by price, timing, competition, capability, or decision-maker access.

### 2. Existing Customer Expansion
- Classify current customers into `Key`, `Growth`, and `Maintain` segments.
- For every key account, define one upsell, one cross-sell, and one renewal protection action per quarter.
- Create account plans for the top 10 revenue customers with named owners and target values.
- Trigger renewal discussions at least 90 days before contract expiry.

### 3. Pricing Improvement
- Identify offerings with strong demand but weak realization.
- Tighten discount approvals so only the business head can sign off on large discounting.
- Build a quarterly pricing review that compares quoted price, won price, margin, and discount percentage.
- Bundle services where the value case is stronger than line-item pricing.

### 4. Delivery Capacity
- Forecast manpower, vendor capacity, and working capital one quarter ahead of target demand.
- Track fulfillment risk for all high-value deals before closing them.
- Review service quality, turnaround time, and client escalations in the monthly operating review.
- Protect retention by giving operations visibility into committed revenue before it is invoiced.

## Operating Rhythm

### Weekly Sales Review
- Review leads added, qualified opportunities, proposals sent, closures, slippages, and blocked deals.
- Compare actual weighted pipeline against the monthly and quarterly floor.
- Escalate all deals above a defined threshold that are stuck for more than 14 days in one stage.
- Confirm owner, next action, and close date for every top deal.

### Monthly Business Review
- Compare actual revenue vs target.
- Review billed revenue, collected revenue, gross margin, DSO, overdue receivables, renewal status, and churn risk.
- Check performance by channel, product line, geography, and top accounts.
- Publish a red-amber-green status for revenue, pipeline, collections, capacity, and margin.

### Quarterly Correction Plan
- Trigger an intervention if revenue is below plan by more than `10%`.
- Increase prospecting intensity and target faster-close offers first.
- Pull forward expansion opportunities from existing customers.
- Freeze non-essential discounting until margin recovery is visible.
- Add temporary delivery support if fulfillment risk is blocking sales.

## Core KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Target Guide |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Revenue achievement | Actual revenue divided by target | `>= 100%` by year-end |
| Weighted pipeline coverage | Weighted pipeline divided by upcoming quarter target | `>= 3.0x` |
| Win rate | Closed won divided by qualified opportunities | Improve quarter over quarter |
| Average deal size | Revenue divided by number of won deals | Stable or rising |
| Existing customer growth | Revenue from current customers vs prior period | Positive each quarter |
| DSO | Average days to collect invoices | Reduce quarter over quarter |
| Overdue receivables | Past-due amount as share of billed revenue | Controlled and reviewed monthly |
| Gross margin | Revenue minus direct cost as share of revenue | Protected despite growth |

## Implementation Steps for April 2026 Start
1. Lock monthly and quarterly targets with owners.
2. Segment all active customers and prospects.
3. Build the next 180-day pipeline with close dates, stage, and probability.
4. Set channel-wise lead targets and weekly outreach goals.
5. Publish pricing guardrails and approval rules.
6. Prepare a capacity plan covering headcount, vendor support, and working capital.
7. Start the weekly sales review and monthly business review from the first week of April 2026.

## Escalation Rules
- If weighted pipeline drops below `2.5x` for the next quarter, increase lead generation immediately.
- If one customer contributes more than `25%` of the quarter target, create a replacement pipeline buffer.
- If DSO worsens for two consecutive months, finance and sales must jointly review payment terms and collection ownership.
- If delivery delays affect more than two key accounts in a month, freeze new stretch commitments until operations stabilizes.

## Success Criteria
- Revenue closes at or above `26.00 cr`.
- No quarter misses target by more than `10%`.
- Collections keep pace with billing and DSO does not materially worsen.
- The business enters FY 2027-28 with a healthy pipeline and retained customer base.
